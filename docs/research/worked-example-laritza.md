# Laritza × "The Honest Income Coach" — A Live Worked Example

*This is what the product would actually output for Laritza Perez, built from the three research briefs and her real resume. It walks her whole journey through every V1 feature. Pay figures are the real numbers the research surfaced (BLS OEWS, AAPC/HFMA/NAHAM, ZipRecruiter/Indeed). The job postings are **illustrative archetypes drawn from the real remote-RCM posting patterns the research found** — not live-scraped listings — so nothing here is fabricated as a specific real job.*

---

## 1. The Diagnosis — she's mislabeled, not unqualified

The product's first job is to tell her the truth about why she's stuck.

**How her resume reads to an ATS / recruiter today:**
> Title bucket detected: **"Customer Service Representative / Administrative Assistant"**
> Pay band for that bucket (remote, national): **~$16–24/hr** → *misses the $30 floor*

**What she actually is, based on her own bullets:**
> Latent bucket: **"Revenue Cycle / Patient Access Specialist"**
> Pay band for that bucket (remote, national): **~$25–34/hr** → *clears or brushes the floor*

**The verdict the app shows her:**
> *"You're not under-qualified — you're under-labeled. You already do eligibility verification, claims, denials, billing, AR follow-up, and EHR work. Recruiters are filing you as 'support' because that's the language on your resume. The gap to $30 is mostly vocabulary and one cheap credential — not years of new skill."*

This is the whole wedge in one screen: **she is the "already 80% qualified, mislabeled" user.**

---

## 2. Resume Re-Frame Studio — before → after

The app rewrites her real experience into the keyword bucket that clears the ATS for $30 roles. **Honesty guardrail: every reframed line is backed by something she actually did — a "defense sheet" she could survive a screen on. No invented skills.**

**New headline (was: none / "Customer Service Professional"):**
> **Revenue Cycle Specialist** — Patient Access · Eligibility & Claims · Denials/AR · Bilingual (EN/ES)

| Her real bullet (before) | Reframed for the $30 bucket (after) | Keywords unlocked |
|---|---|---|
| "Verified insurance coverage and processed claims accurately and efficiently" | "Performed **insurance eligibility & benefits verification** and end-to-end **claims processing** across payer portals in a high-volume remote queue" | eligibility verification · benefits verification · claims · payer portals |
| "Resolved billing discrepancies and maintained electronic health records" | "Owned **billing-discrepancy resolution and AR follow-up**; maintained **EHR** data integrity and documentation" | AR follow-up · denials · revenue cycle · EHR |
| "Delivered phone-based support to patients and providers" | "Served as **patient access** point of contact for patients and providers, coordinating **prior-auth and benefits** questions bilingually" | patient access · prior authorization · bilingual |
| "Managed client accounts, billing reviews, and payment follow-ups remotely" | "Managed **revenue-cycle account follow-up**, **payment posting** review, and remote **collections** workflows" | payment posting · collections · revenue cycle |

**What changed:** zero new claims, but her resume now surfaces ~12 of the exact keywords RCM postings screen for. The app instruments **callback rate** before/after — so if the reframe *doesn't* lift callbacks, it tells her, instead of pretending it worked.

## 4. Laritza's Income Staircase — the honest path to $30+

Not "$30 in 12 months." A staircase with a real raise up front and substantiated ranges at each rung.

| Step | When | Move | Outcome (real research numbers) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 — Reframe & target** | Week 1–4 | Resume re-frame + apply only to in-bucket, real, ≥$25 remote roles | More callbacks · **$0 spent** |
| **1 — The bridge** | Month 0–3 | Land a *titled* Patient Access / Eligibility / Denials role | **~$25–29/hr** — a real raise over a likely current ~$20–24/hr, and the right title on her resume |
| **2 — The $30 rung** | Month 6–24 | **CHAA** ($179, NAHAM) + **CRCR** (~$400, HFMA) + on-the-job **Epic** exposure | **Revenue Cycle Analyst → $30–34/hr** (remote Epic ~$31.60; FL 75th pct $33.65) ✅ |
| **3 — The ceiling** | Month 24–36 | Senior RC Analyst, or add Salesforce **Health Cloud** (she already uses Salesforce) | **$35–45/hr** |

**Backup track, run in parallel (free):** reframe toward **Health-tech CSM / Implementation** ($30–34/hr) — her bilingual + EHR + Salesforce story fits. Near-zero cost; convert the on-ramp from inside rather than declining winnable offers.

**The honesty box the app always shows:**
> *"Realistic month-12 outcome for your profile: a titled $26–29/hr bridge role. Sustained $30+ typically lands around month 18–24. CHAA/CRCR help you pass screens — they don't guarantee a hire; the bridge experience is what converts them. Total credential cost ~$554, and we'll point you to free WIOA funding before you pay a dollar. If your chosen path stalls, we'll tell you it stalled — not quietly slide your dates."*

That paragraph is the entire brand: realistic, hedged, no guarantee, no ISA, points her to *free* money first — the things an outcome-financed competitor literally cannot say.
